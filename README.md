I'm a Computer Science undergraduate at ShanghaiTech University and a research assistant in METAL Lab. My current work combines energy-harvesting circuits, low-power firmware, on-device machine learning, and system measurements. I usually work from schematic and PCB through firmware, model deployment, and validation.

Since 2025, I've also been president of [GeekPie Association](https://github.com/ShanghaitechGeekPie), ShanghaiTech's student technology society. I organize workshops, shared lab resources, project teams, and campus technology events.

## What I'm working on

**PEAK** is a self-powered vibration sensor built around the nRF54L15. I designed its energy-harvesting power board, firmware, feature pipeline, TinyML model, and measurement setup. It performs sensing, inference, and BLE transmission using harvested energy.

**Battery-free IoT energy management** is the subject of my recent research with METAL Lab. Our work received the Best Paper Award at IEEE ICPS 2026, where I was a co-first author.

**LoongArch, FPGA, and Linux** are my current systems work for NSCSCC 2026. I'm refactoring a contest CPU in SpinalHDL and working on Reduced Linux bring-up for the FPGA platform. Some of this work remains private while the competition is active.

## Things I've built

- **[BetterEAMS](https://github.com/Maotechh/BetterEAMS):** a Tampermonkey userscript that makes ShanghaiTech course selection easier to search, plan, and manage.
- **[Vivado on Parallels ARM64](https://github.com/Maotechh/vivado-parallels-arm64-rosetta):** installer and compatibility scripts for running x86_64 AMD Vivado inside an Ubuntu ARM64 virtual machine on Apple Silicon, including XSim and JTAG support.
- **[VEX Communication](https://github.com/Maotechh/VEX_communication):** hardware notes, a small PCB, and example code for connecting a VEX Brain V5 to Arduino, Raspberry Pi, or ESP32 over RS-485.
- **[Lab Management Copilot](https://github.com/Maotechh/Lab-Management-Copilot):** a web application for laboratory inventory, natural-language stock operations, alerts, and experiment preparation lists.

## Tools and platforms

For embedded work, I use C/C++, Python, nRF54, ESP32, STM32, Zephyr, TensorFlow Lite Micro, BLE, and PlatformIO. For digital systems, I use Verilog, SpinalHDL, Vivado, and Linux. I document with Typst and LaTeX, and automate with Git, Docker, and shell scripts.
